another-meaningless-ai-spew

2026-07-16· 5 min read

We are back, and you'll never guess the topic of today's post. I wanted to write this like a week ago and talk about how the closed frontier is back and America stays on top, but I got lazy. Then yesterday I wanted to write about how Open Weight has clapped back again, and America comes back in Open Weight, but again I got lazy. And now I am writing it, with China being back. I don't know why I am justifying my lack of consistency to my 0-person viewer base, but anyway, what a time to be alive this is genuinely cinema.

To start is the first thing I was going to talk about, the closed frontier. A lot of benchmark gaps but I had to work with what I had.

OpenAI's 5.6 family killed it in benchmarks and went into hiding just to finally release and actually be a great model. Plus we got more cinema of Sama and Elon beefing on X. Speaking of X, Zuck posted on X for the first time in years for the release of his latest closed model, Muse Spark 1.1, a crazy cheap model with surprisingly high capability, especially in coding. That same day, Cursor/Xai/SpaceX/SpaceXAI released Grok 4.5, which delivered near-frontier capabilities at a low inference cost. And oh my god it is fast. I personally have been using a stack of GPT-5.6 Sol and Grok 4.5 Fast in Cursor, and it has been amazing. I have not thought of Anthropic in a while (they will be back). GPT-5.6 Sol is such a smart model at literally everything and is cheaper than Fable. Still might be overkill for some of the stuff I'm doing, but oh well. On the other hand Grok 4.5 Fast is so fucking fast (who would have thought) and is really good. I have been using it as a research subagent in plans that I make with 5.6 sol, and I've also been using it for implementations, anytime I want to just chat with the AI and build context or have it explain something to me. I'm having so much fun with it that I feel bad delegating it to the subagent role because it really doesn't deserve it. I always knew Elon wasn't going to stay a compute salesman, but I was starting to lose faith. I'm really surprised there isn't more chatter around this but then again, does any model get chatter when the new frontier model is always a day or two out. These were genuinely some of the craziest days we've seen in AI history and it genuinely felt like the labs made a solid push and gave the frontier a safe lead. Sam and Dario had untouchable levels of intelligence, and Elon and Zuck had cheap high quality tokens. What could go wrong?

Open source is always lurking I guess but not in the way I expected. Thinking Machines Lab releasing a roughly 1T parameter model was not on my 2026 scorecard. This is the same company that raised a 2B at 10B pre-product seed then was called a failure after releasing its product, Tinker, in October then losing multiple cofounders and basically considered dead. But I guess Mira Murati is really like that because wow. Now this isn't frontier-level intelligence by any means.

It is clearly a sub Sonnet/GLM 5.2 model but it still feels very important. With the landscape of Chinese open weight models being potentially blocked in the US, depending on what the Chinese government decides, America would lose access to high-quality open intelligence. Until now, there were really no other players keeping us in this. But that's not why I think Mira Murati is going to win. I am super bullish on Thinking Machines Lab because I see them as a company delivering personal AI solutions to enterprises. It feels like the infrastructure is being built out as we speak, with Tinker being the lab to customize models. Pairing that with a real, viable open weight model opens up so many doors for business through tuning and post training models for enterprise customers as well as implementing for them. Obviously, this works in a use case where enterprises want custom models on proprietary data, etc. But that isn't a reality right now since general intelligence is getting the job done more than well enough and this would really only ever exist in a world where the frontier stopped growing at the rate we expect it to, but nevertheless, I am intrigued by the bet that Mira Murati is making, and I love to see America starting to win in open weight again.

Well America was starting to win in open weight for about a day, and then came Kimi K3.

This is quite the model. Take the DeepSWE benchmark with a grain of salt since it isn't SWE-Bench-Pro. But still for an open weight model, holy shit. I was going crazy when GLM 5.2 was touching Sonnet numbers, now we're moving into the Opus class and even pushing the Fable/Sol class. This is already a pretty crazy release, then you see that the cost is significantly cheaper than Opus. It is genuinely inconceivable how they are doing it. Moonshot AI is a 20 billion dollar company in comparison to OpenAI and Anthropic's roughly trillion dollar valuations. On top of that, the frontier labs have access to the top-of-the-line Nvidia training clusters, while the Chinese companies have to train on the lagging Huawei chips. It genuinely blows my mind how they are continuing to fight these labs head-on because they are completely set up to lose. And I really do believe that China will catch up on the chips, will invest in the training clusters that Americans have, and will train massive models at scale (not that Kimi K3 isn't massive), and they will beat America at this rate. It is genuinely so scary how they do it and Frontier Labs have to iterate like they never have. They maintain an edge for now but who knows what will happen when the next crazy model drops in 1-2 business days.

Anyway I just wasted an hour writing all this, and I don't know why I have nothing to talk about besides for LLMs. I need to figure out a new topic for the next post. Not that anyone is reading this.
