The paradigm shift that we are facing with open weight models performing on par with frontier labs' is real and exciting with Z.ai’s GLM 5.2 leading the charge. It has been 4.5 months since Anthropic released Sonnet 4.6 and they finally released Sonnet 5.0; a comparable model to GLM 5.2, yet far behind in costs. GLM 5.2 has roughly identical benchmark numbers to Sonnet 5.0 at less than half the price for output tokens.

![](/assets/blog/screenshot-2026-06-30-at-4-37-26-pm-mr1j7xt2.png)

This is a scary reality check for Anthropic and all of the US closed labs. The value of being a closed lab lies mainly in the profitability from inference tokens as well as the protection of the company's IP to stay on the cutting edge. When the open labs are eating into both of these it's a pretty telling sign that their lead is not safe. The even more concerning part for them is that the Chinese open models are trained for the most part on non-SOTA Huawei chips (most part since there could have been some smuggled NVIDIA GPUs in the mix who knows). As the Chinese chip technology catches up to the US, it's hard to imagine that these models won't see major strides in performance.

I'm interested to see how AI climate shifts with the open surge. One group of stakeholders that benefits significantly from this is the cloud providers. Running these models is great for their margins, because unlike with the closed models, they don't have to pay anything to the open model providers. It's worth considering that if they can profit more from the open models, they could begin to favor them and optimize inference for them, which would be an incredible negative blow for the frontier labs in an already extremely compute-constrained climate. The other stakeholders who's behavior could show a drastic change is the enterprise customers. We've just exited from the tokenmaxxing honeymoon that companies had been having with AI and company leaders are realizing that lots of usage is wildly inefficient. This is leading to the era of using AI through orchestrator layers, which intelligently pick the model for a given prompt or job to optimize the balance of cost and model power. This is also a time where ROI on AI usage will be highly scrutinized, meaning that enterprises will have an easy choice when picking between two similar models with drastic cost differences.

On top of the labs, a stakeholder that should be extremely worried is the United States government. Open source is led by China with the leaders being Z.ai's GLM models, Deepseek's models, Moonshot AI's Kimi models, and Alibaba's Qwen models; all chinese companies. With America's biggest competition leading the push that can very realistically disrupt the US lead in AI, change needs to be made. It does not help that any SOTA model now will be released witht he fear that it is almost guaranteed to be blocked from public use by the US government. Although they just made strides towards lifting the Fable block, multiple GPT models still remain in regulatory purgatory. This is horrible when you need innovation in the space to compete with the global forces. The answer to this is a clear regulatory framework, so that model providers can build models that they know will not get blocked and can do so confidently and innovate freely. This must be implemented as soon as possible and feels like it is already overdue. We do have some open source movement through the NVIDIA Nemotron family, the Google Gemma family as well as others, but these realistically don't hold a candle to the top Chinese open models.

I think that the open push is one of the most interesting points in this AI journey so far and will really shape the future of the industry and world. Open weight/source is particularly interesting for innovation, as people can continue to build upon them even externally. It is also extremely valuable for the democratization of AI because cheap AI is accessible AI. I think the labs are sitting at an inflection point where more than ever, they need to show their value and justify their prices or change their strategy. Otherwise, the winners may be the ones who nobody could have expected from the East. Obviously, it is necessary to note that I've mostly made comparisons to the Sonnet model family, when Anthropic has the Opus and Fable family, and OpenAI has the GPT 5.6 Sol and Terra models, which still remain a step/steps above these open models. So I'd like to end on the core point that China is definitely not there yet with top-end model capabilities, but they are surely catching up and the way that the US and the frontier labs handle this shift is going to be very important in shaping the future of AI.
